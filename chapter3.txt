Chapter 3 – The Rift in the Woods
The forest shimmered unnaturally now, its trees bending in directions trees shouldn’t bend. Static crackled in the air. The tech-lenses on their helmets highlighted anomalies—energy flows, heat signatures, and flickering figures between dimensions.

The further they moved, the more Earth seemed to blur.

“The rift is near,” said Lena, voice distorted through her communicator. Marcus gripped a plasma rod the alien had handed him.

The glade, as marked on the holographic map, revealed itself—floating stones circled an ancient relic glowing with deep violet light.

Suddenly, the wind stopped. A low hum began to vibrate in their bones.

“We’re not alone,” Marina whispered.
